# Spellsinger Rework - Design Document

## Current State

The Spellsinger is a hybrid mage-bard adventurer class with:
- TRAIT_ARCYNE_T2, TRAIT_EMPATH, TRAIT_GOODLOVER
- 14 spellpoints (legacy system, being deprecated)
- BARD_T2 inspiration (audience of 4, picks 2 songs from T1/T2 pools)
- Journeyman swords, Expert music, Apprentice arcane
- Mockery spell + Arcyne Forge
- Sabre + instrument of choice

### Problems
1. **Passive gameplay**: Pick songs, play instrument, AFK. No moment-to-moment decisions.
2. **Identity crisis**: Worse bard than Cantor/Psyaltrist (no miracles, same T2 songs), worse mage than Sorcerer (only 14 spellpoints vs full aspect system).
3. **Spellpoints are legacy**: The class needs a resource system that matches modern Azure Peak design (momentum, aspects, devotion).
4. **No combat loop**: Spellblade has "hit things -> build momentum -> spend momentum on abilities." Spellsinger has "play music and hope your party carries."

### Comparison Points
- **Cantor**: BARD_T2 + CLERIC_T2 miracles + Mockery + dodge expert. Divine bard. Songs fuel by devotion regen.
- **Psyaltrist**: BARD_T3 + CLERIC_T2 miracles + Mockery + Master music. The ultimate church bard.
- **Minstrel**: BARD_T3 + no combat. Pure civilian musician.
- **Spellblade**: Arcyne Momentum system. Melee builds stacks, abilities consume stacks. Proven, fun, interactive.

### Design Constraints
- Cantor must remain the better pure bard (T2 bard + miracles > anything Spellsinger gets as a bard).
- Spellsinger should not be a better mage than Sorcerer (no full aspect system).
- The instrument must remain central to gameplay - this is a bard, not a wizard who happens to own a lute.
- Must use a modern resource system, not spellpoints.

## Proposal A: Resonance Weaver (Sona-inspired Aura Switching)

### Core Mechanic: Resonance

The Spellsinger maintains a **Resonance** resource (0-8 stacks, displayed as a status effect). Resonance is generated by **playing music near other players** - every pulse of an active song grants 1 Resonance if at least 1 audience member is within range. Resonance decays after 12 seconds of not gaining any (1 stack per 6 seconds, same rate as arcyne momentum).

The Spellsinger has three **Motifs** they can switch between freely (no cooldown, instant switch). Each Motif modifies their passive aura and changes what their **Coda** ability does. After using any Motif ability, their next melee attack within 6 seconds is empowered (Power Chord - bonus damage + effect based on active Motif).

**This replaces both spellpoints and the standard bardic songbook.** The Spellsinger does NOT pick songs from the T1/T2/T3 learnable lists. Instead, their three Motifs ARE their songs, always active while playing.

### Bard Tier: BARD_T1 (not T2)

The Spellsinger drops to BARD_T1 inspiration. They get a max audience of 2 (down from 4) and pick 1 song from the T1 list only. This is strictly worse than Cantor's BARD_T2 (audience 4, picks from T1+T2). The tradeoff: the Spellsinger's Motif system provides unique effects that no standard bard song can replicate, but they affect fewer people and lack the raw support power of a dedicated bard.

### Abilities

| Ability | Type | Cost | Cooldown | Description |
|---------|------|------|----------|-------------|
| **Motif: Valor** | Toggle (Instant) | None | 3s switch CD | Active Motif. While playing and this is active, audience members within 7 tiles gain +1 STR. Power Chord (next melee): Deal 15 bonus damage and grant the target's attacker (you) a 3-second +10% parry chance buff. |
| **Motif: Elegy** | Toggle (Instant) | None | 3s switch CD | Active Motif. While playing and this is active, non-audience members within 7 tiles suffer -1 SPD. Power Chord (next melee): Deal 10 bonus damage and apply a 4-second slow (minor knockdown-speed penalty, not a real knockdown). |
| **Motif: Grace** | Toggle (Instant) | None | 3s switch CD | Active Motif. While playing and this is active, audience members within 7 tiles regenerate 0.3 stamina/tick. Power Chord (next melee): Deal 10 bonus damage and heal yourself for 5 brute/burn. |
| **Coda** | Targeted, 5-tile range | 4 Resonance | 18s | Unleash a burst of arcyne sound at a target. Effect depends on active Motif: **Valor Coda** - 30 brute damage sonic blast. **Elegy Coda** - 3-second stun (blocked by spell guard). **Grace Coda** - Heal target for 15 brute+burn and remove 1 wound. |
| **Dissonance** | Self, AoE 3 tiles | 6 Resonance | 30s | Emergency: stop playing, release a shockwave of clashing sound. All non-audience within 3 tiles are knocked back 2 tiles and take 20 brute. All audience within 3 tiles gain 5 seconds of +2 SPD. Puts instrument on a 10-second "retuning" cooldown before you can play again. |
| **Mockery** | Inherited | None | Existing | Kept as-is from current Spellsinger. |

### Instrument Interaction
- Motif auras ONLY work while actively playing an instrument. Sheathe the instrument, lose the aura.
- Resonance ONLY generates while playing near audience. No audience, no Resonance.
- Dissonance forcibly stops your music and locks you out for 10 seconds - it is a panic button, not a rotation ability.
- Power Chord requires swapping from instrument to weapon (put instrument away, swing sword, Power Chord procs, then you can pull instrument back out). This creates the Sona-like weave: play -> swap -> hit -> swap -> play.

### Cantor vs. Spellsinger Under This Proposal
| Feature | Cantor | Spellsinger (Resonance) |
|---------|--------|------------------------|
| Bard Tier | T2 (audience 4, T1+T2 songs) | T1 (audience 2, T1 song only) |
| Miracles | CLERIC_T2 | None |
| Combat Spells | Mockery only | Motifs + Coda + Dissonance + Mockery |
| Melee | Journeyman knives, dodge expert | Journeyman swords |
| Support Power | High (larger audience, better songs, healing miracles) | Moderate (smaller audience, unique but weaker auras) |
| Solo Power | Low | Moderate (Power Chord melee, Coda burst, Dissonance escape) |

### Strengths
- Skirmisher identity: weave between playing and fighting
- Always has something to do - switch motifs, proc Power Chords, build toward Coda
- Rewards positioning (stay near audience for Resonance, stay near enemies for Elegy debuff)
- Dissonance is a satisfying "oh no" button

### Weaknesses
- Worse pure support than Cantor (smaller audience, no miracles, weaker song selection)
- Weapon-swapping between instrument and sabre is clunky if BYOND input lag is bad
- Resonance generation requires allies nearby - solo Spellsinger is resource-starved
- Vulnerable while playing (one hand occupied)

### Why It Would Be Fun
The constant motif-switching creates a rhythm: Valor for combat phases, Grace when your team needs recovery, Elegy to debuff enemies before a fight. Power Chord rewards you for dipping into melee instead of standing in the back. Coda is a satisfying payoff for sustained play. You feel like a battle-musician, not a jukebox.

## Proposal B: Chordweaver (Echo/Combo System)

### Core Mechanic: Chord Stacking

The Spellsinger has four **Notes** - arcyne tones that can be woven together. Each Note is a low-cooldown ability (8 seconds) that applies a minor effect on its own. But the real power comes from **Chords**: when you play 2 Notes within 6 seconds, they combine into a Chord with a much stronger effect. The order matters - Note A then Note B produces a different Chord than Note B then Note A.

Notes are played by activating the ability while holding an instrument (they consume energy like standard songs). A visible "note trail" appears above the Spellsinger showing which notes are active (colored particles matching each note).

**No Resonance, no momentum, no stacks.** The resource here is cooldown management and note sequencing. The skill expression is in choosing the right 2-note combo for the situation.

### Bard Tier: BARD_T1

Same reasoning as Proposal A. Drops to T1, audience 2, 1 T1 song pick. Standard bard songs still work alongside Notes (you can play a melody AND weave Notes).

### The Four Notes

| Note | Color | Solo Effect (minor) | Energy Cost |
|------|-------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Forte** (F) | Red | 15 brute damage to target in 5 tiles | 20 |
| **Piano** (P) | Blue | Heal self for 8 brute+burn | 15 |
| **Staccato** (S) | Gold | +1 SPD to self for 8 seconds | 15 |
| **Grave** (G) | Purple | -1 PER to target in 5 tiles for 8 seconds | 15 |

### Chord Combinations (First Note -> Second Note)

| Chord | Notes | Cooldown | Effect |
|-------|-------|----------|--------|
| **Crescendo** | F -> F | 20s | 35 brute AoE (3 tiles around target). "The music swells to a deafening peak!" |
| **Sforzando** | F -> S | 16s | Dash 3 tiles toward target, deal 25 brute on arrival. Similar to a mini-Caedo. |
| **Requiem** | F -> G | 16s | 20 brute + 4-second silence (target cannot cast spells). "A funeral march for their power." |
| **Cantabile** | P -> P | 20s | Heal target ally for 20 brute+burn + remove 1 wound. Must be in audience. |
| **Lullaby** | P -> G | 16s | 3-second drowsy on target (screen blur, -2 PER). Blocked by spell guard. |
| **Serenade** | P -> S | 16s | Grant audience member within 7 tiles a 6-second shield absorbing 15 damage. |
| **Vivace** | S -> S | 20s | Self: +3 SPD, +20% dodge for 6 seconds. "Feet blur with impossible speed!" |
| **Marcia** | S -> F | 16s | Audience within 7 tiles gain +15% bypass parry for 8 seconds. War march. |
| **Dirge** | G -> G | 20s | AoE 5 tiles: all non-audience lose 20 stamina + -1 WIL for 10 seconds. |
| **Elegy** | G -> P | 16s | Self: 6 seconds of lifesteal on melee hits (heal 30% of damage dealt). |
| **Fugue** | G -> F | 16s | Mark target for 8 seconds. Audience members deal +10 damage to marked target. |
| **Presto** | S -> G | 16s | Teleport-swap positions with target within 5 tiles (ally or enemy). Both disoriented 1 second. |

### Instrument Interaction
- Notes can ONLY be played while holding an instrument (does not need to be actively playing music, but instrument must be in hand).
- If you are actively playing a standard bard song, Notes layer on top - you keep the song going.
- Chords produce a visible+audible flourish (the two note colors merge in a particle burst).
- Instrument type has no mechanical effect on Notes, only flavor.

### Abilities Beyond Notes

| Ability | Type | Cost | Cooldown | Description |
|---------|------|------|----------|-------------|
| **Overture** | Self | 40 energy | 45s | For 10 seconds, all Note cooldowns are halved (4s instead of 8s) and Chord cooldowns reduced by 5s. Visual: golden glow + tempo-increase sound. |
| **Mockery** | Inherited | None | Existing | Kept as-is. |

### Cantor vs. Spellsinger Under This Proposal
| Feature | Cantor | Spellsinger (Chordweaver) |
|---------|--------|--------------------------|
| Bard Tier | T2 (audience 4, T1+T2 songs) | T1 (audience 2, T1 song only) |
| Miracles | CLERIC_T2 | None |
| Combat Kit | Mockery, daggers | 12 Chord combos + Overture + Mockery, swords |
| Complexity | Low (pick song, play, done) | High (memorize combos, sequence Notes) |
| Support | Broad (large audience, strong songs) | Targeted (small audience, specific Chord effects) |

### Strengths
- Extremely high skill ceiling - 12 different Chords means you always have options
- Every situation has an optimal combo, rewarding game knowledge
- Can flex between offense (F combos), defense (P combos), utility (S/G combos)
- Feels like playing a real instrument - sequences of inputs producing different results
- Overture creates exciting "go time" windows

### Weaknesses
- High learning curve - 12 combos to memorize is a lot
- Energy-hungry if you spam Notes without thinking
- Chord window (6 seconds) is tight under pressure
- Without Overture, long stretches where you are waiting on cooldowns
- Still needs instrument in hand, limiting weapon uptime

### Why It Would Be Fun
This is the "fighting game character" of bards. Every fight is a puzzle - what Chord do I need right now? Sforzando to gap-close, then Requiem to silence the enemy mage, then Cantabile to heal my friend. The 6-second combo window adds tension without being punishing. Overture turns you into a machine gun of musical effects for 10 glorious seconds. Mastery feels earned, not given.

## Proposal C: Hymnsinger (Momentum-Resonance Hybrid)

### Core Mechanic: Harmonic Momentum

This proposal directly adapts the proven Spellblade momentum system but reskins it for a bard. Instead of melee strikes building stacks, **playing music near people** builds **Harmonic Momentum** (0-8 stacks). Every pulse of your active song (roughly every 10 ticks while playing) grants 1 stack if at least 1 person (audience or not) is within hearing range. Melee strikes ALSO grant 1 stack (2-second internal cooldown, same as Spellblade).

Harmonic Momentum decays after 10 seconds of no gain, losing 1 stack per 5 seconds. Getting stunned or knocked down dumps all stacks (same as Spellblade). The overcharge threshold is at 7 stacks - above that, you take 2 damage per tick and start visibly crackling, but your abilities gain empowered effects.

The Spellsinger gets a fixed kit of 4 abilities that spend Harmonic Momentum, plus Mockery.

### Bard Tier: BARD_T1

Drops to T1 like the other proposals. Audience 2, 1 T1 song pick.

### Abilities

| Ability | Cost | Cooldown | Base Effect | Empowered (7+ stacks before spending) |
|---------|------|----------|-------------|---------------------------------------|
| **Rallying Refrain** | 2 stacks | 12s | Audience members within 7 tiles gain +1 STR and +1 CON for 10 seconds. | Also grants +1 SPD and the buff lasts 15 seconds. |
| **Discordant Strike** | 3 stacks | 15s | Your next melee attack within 8 seconds deals +20 bonus damage and bypasses parry. Must have weapon in hand (not instrument). | +30 bonus damage instead, and the hit applies a 3-second -2 SPD slow. |
| **Wanderer's Step** | 2 stacks | 10s | Blink 3 tiles in the direction you're facing. Leaves a brief afterimage. | Blink 4 tiles and leave a 2-second sound decoy at your origin (enemies briefly see a "ghost" of you playing music). |
| **Magnum Opus** | 6 stacks (all consumed) | 40s | Must be playing music. AoE 5 tiles: Audience members are healed for 15 brute+burn and gain 4s of +20% dodge. Non-audience take 25 brute sonic damage and are knocked back 1 tile. | Healing increased to 25, damage increased to 35, knockback increased to 2 tiles, and the bard gains 5 seconds of damage immunity after casting. |
| **Mockery** | None | Existing | Kept as-is. | N/A |

### Instrument Interaction
- Harmonic Momentum generates from playing music (primary source) AND melee hits (secondary source).
- This creates a natural rhythm: play to build stacks -> spend stacks on Rallying Refrain or prep Discordant Strike -> swap to weapon -> hit with empowered strike -> swap back to instrument -> resume building.
- Magnum Opus requires actively playing - it is the "I've been building up to this" moment.
- Wanderer's Step works regardless of what you're holding - it is your escape tool.

### Harmonic Momentum Implementation Notes

Uses the same `/datum/status_effect/buff/arcyne_momentum` base but with a subtype:

```
/datum/status_effect/buff/arcyne_momentum/harmonic
    max_stacks = 8
    glow_colour = "#d4a843"    // warm gold instead of blue
    crackle_colour = "#c92f2f" // red crackling at overcharge
    overcharge_threshold = 7
    overcharge_damage = 2      // less punishing than spellblade's 4
    melee_gain_cooldown = 2 SECONDS
```

Additional stack source: register for the playing_music status effect to tick harmonic momentum. This is new code but follows existing signal patterns.

### Cantor vs. Spellsinger Under This Proposal
| Feature | Cantor | Spellsinger (Hymnsinger) |
|---------|--------|--------------------------|
| Bard Tier | T2 (audience 4, T1+T2 songs) | T1 (audience 2, T1 song only) |
| Miracles | CLERIC_T2 | None |
| Resource | Devotion (passive regen) | Harmonic Momentum (active generation) |
| Burst | Low | High (Magnum Opus, Discordant Strike) |
| Sustain | High (miracles + songs) | Moderate (must keep playing to maintain momentum) |
| Mobility | Low | Moderate (Wanderer's Step) |

### Strengths
- Leverages a proven system (Spellblade momentum) - lower implementation risk
- Clear gameplay loop: play music -> build stacks -> spend on abilities -> repeat
- Dual generation (music + melee) means you're always progressing even in combat
- Magnum Opus is a huge satisfying moment - the "drop the beat" feeling
- Overcharge creates tension: do I dump stacks now or risk the self-damage for empowered effects?
- Wanderer's Step gives the "annoying skirmisher" feel from the Sona reference

### Weaknesses
- Fewer abilities than Proposal B (4 vs 12 combos) - less variety
- Momentum generation from music is somewhat passive (play and wait)
- Overcharge threshold at 7/8 means empowered effects require sitting at dangerous stacks
- Less unique than Proposals A/B - could feel like "Spellblade but with a lute"
- Magnum Opus's 40s cooldown + 6-stack cost means it is rare - most fights might end before you get to use it

### Why It Would Be Fun
This is the most accessible of the three proposals. You don't need to memorize combos or manage three toggles. Play music, watch the number go up, spend it on cool things. The momentum system is already proven to feel good on Spellblade - adapting it for a bard preserves that "building toward something" satisfaction. The overcharge decision point (stay at 7+ for empowered effects vs. dump early for safety) creates meaningful tension every fight. Wanderer's Step with the sound decoy at empowered is the kind of trickster moment that makes people laugh.

## Summary Comparison

| Criteria | A: Resonance Weaver | B: Chordweaver | C: Hymnsinger |
|----------|---------------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Complexity** | Medium | High | Low-Medium |
| **Skill Ceiling** | Medium-High | Very High | Medium |
| **Fun Floor** | High (motifs feel good immediately) | Medium (need to learn combos) | High (momentum is intuitive) |
| **Implementation Effort** | Medium (new status effect, 3 motif toggles, coda, dissonance) | High (4 note abilities, 12 chord outcomes, combo tracker, overture) | Low-Medium (subtype existing momentum, 4 abilities) |
| **Skirmisher Fantasy** | Strong (motif swap + Power Chord weaving) | Moderate (more caster-like) | Strong (momentum + Wanderer's Step) |
| **Support Capability** | Moderate (auras are weaker but constant) | Moderate-High (flexible Chord heals/buffs) | Moderate (Rallying Refrain, Magnum Opus heal) |
| **Solo Viability** | Low (needs audience for Resonance) | Medium (Notes work solo, Chords are self-sufficient) | Medium (melee also generates momentum) |
| **Risk of "AFK Bard"** | Low (must swap instruments/weapon) | Very Low (constant input required) | Low (must actively manage momentum) |
| **Cantor Stays Better Bard?** | Yes (T2 > T1, miracles > motifs for pure support) | Yes (T2 > T1, miracles > chords for healing) | Yes (T2 > T1, miracles > momentum for sustain) |

## Recommendation

**Proposal A (Resonance Weaver)** offers the best balance of accessibility and depth. It has the highest "fun floor" - even a new player understands "switch modes, hit things between songs." The motif system is immediately intuitive (red = fight, blue = debuff, green = heal) and Power Chord creates the weapon-swapping skirmisher fantasy that matches the Sona inspiration. Implementation complexity is moderate and the status effect pattern is well-established in the codebase.

**Proposal C (Hymnsinger)** is the safest bet if implementation time is a concern. It reuses the most existing code and the momentum system is battle-tested. It would be a solid, fun class even if it lacks the novelty of the other proposals.

**Proposal B (Chordweaver)** is the most ambitious and would be the most rewarding to master, but risks alienating players who don't want to memorize a combo chart. Best suited if the playerbase trends toward high-skill players who enjoy optimization. Consider it for a future iteration after one of the simpler proposals ships and proves the Spellsinger can be fun.
